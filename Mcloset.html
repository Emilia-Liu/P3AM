Summary of the story

In a futuristic scenario, there are human beings living on Venus.

The children who have lived there since they were born have never seen the sun.

On Venus it rains for seven years and the sun only appears for two hours.

Margot is a different child. Unlike the others, she has lived on planet Earth for five years and remembers the sun. Whenever Margot records the sun and the sensations it brings her, the other children accuse her of lying.

Until the day comes when the sun appears again. The children, jealous, lock Margot in the closet. Meanwhile it stops raining and the sun comes out.

Two hours later it starts raining again. At that moment they remember Margot locked in the closet.

About the author

Ray Douglas Bradbury was an American author and screenwriter.

One of the most celebrated American writers of the 20th century, he worked in a variety of modes, including fantasy, science fiction, horror, mystery, and realistic fiction.

Ray Bradbury was born on August 22, 1920 in Waukegan, Illinois. He began his career in writing at the age of 12. His first publication in 1936 is the poem "In memory of Villa Rogers".

In 1937, Bradbury joined the "Science Fiction League" of Los Angeles. In 1939-1940 he published the mimeograph magazine Futuria Fantasy.

Bradbury wrote eleven novels, the most popular of which were the first: Martian Chronicles (1950), 451 Degrees Fahrenheit (1953) and Dandelion Wine (1957). The writer has also created 21 plays and 28 film scripts.

Between 1985 and 1992, Ray Bradbury authored and hosted a television series of 65 mini-films based on his stories.

Bradbury has received many awards in the field of literature and art in general. He died on June 5, 2012 in Los Angeles at the age of 91.
